# Peer feedback — questions for a free legal advice session

_For a free business-advice/legal clinic (e.g. Strathclyde Inspire, TechScaler,
Barclays Eagle Labs, Royal Bank/NatWest Accelerator). Bring this one-pager.
Not legal advice — written to get you the right answers efficiently._

## What we're building (30-second version)
Plistic is a directory of Scotland's creative/media businesses. We want a **peer
trust** feature where businesses that have worked together can:
1. **Publicly** show confirmed collaborations ("worked with" — both sides agree). *(low concern)*
2. **Privately** give structured feedback on a collaborator (would-work-again + 1–5 scores + a note),
   seen **only by us as the platform**, never published. *(the thing to check)*
3. **Possibly later**, show a public **aggregate** peer score (e.g. "62% would work again, n=11"),
   only to logged-in businesses, above a volume threshold, with a right of reply. *(the sensitive bit)*

We will **not** publish individual named negative reviews or free text.

## The questions
1. **Defamation.** For (3), is an **aggregate, opinion-framed** peer score (no individual
   identifiable, above a volume threshold, right of reply, shown only to logged-in businesses)
   a defensible approach under the Defamation Act 2013 (honest opinion / serious harm /
   the s5 website-operator process)? What wording/framing reduces risk most?
2. **Keeping it private (2).** If negative feedback is **only ever seen by us** (the operator) and
   used internally to decide who we feature/recommend — never shown to anyone else — what's our
   exposure? Any risk in *acting* on it (e.g. not featuring someone)?
3. **Right of reply / takedown.** What process should we offer a rated business (notice, reply,
   dispute, removal)? Is a documented notice-and-takedown enough to rely on the operator defence?
4. **GDPR / data protection.** Feedback about a business is often about an **identifiable person**
   (sole traders, named owners). What's our lawful basis, and how do we handle transparency and
   subject-access / erasure requests for opinions others have left?
5. **Terms of Service.** What clauses do we need — acceptable-use, opinion-not-fact, indemnity,
   our right to remove, limitation of liability?
6. **Overall.** Given we're pre-revenue and can't yet afford ongoing legal support, what's the
   **lowest-risk version** that still lets us capture honest negative signal usefully?

## What's already live vs waiting on this
- **Live & low-risk:** public "worked with" (both sides confirm); private feedback visible only to us.
- **Waiting on your steer:** any **public** display of peer scores (built to stay OFF until signed off).

## Our instinct (sanity-check this)
Keep negatives **private to the platform** (safe, still useful for curation); if we ever go public,
only an **aggregate above a threshold**, opinion-framed, verified-audience, with right of reply.
